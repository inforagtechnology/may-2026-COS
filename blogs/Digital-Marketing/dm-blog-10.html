Everyone Is Scrolling Social Media All Day — So Why Are Companies Struggling to Find People Who Can Actually Market on It?

Here is something that will make you stop and think.

Published by Code Of School Digital Marketing and Tech Career Experts

Table of Contents

The average Indian spends nearly two and a half hours on social media every single day. Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, and X are open on phones across every city, every age group, and every income bracket. Social media is not just entertainment anymore. It is where people discover brands, make purchasing decisions, follow their interests, and shape their identities.

And yet, when companies across India post job openings for Social Media Managers and Social Media Marketing Executives, they struggle to fill those roles with people who genuinely know what they are doing.

Not because the interest is missing. But because most people who consume social media have never learned how to use it as a professional marketing tool. They know how to scroll. They do not know how to strategise, build audiences, run campaigns, analyze performance, and deliver measurable business results.

That gap, between casual user and skilled professional, is one of the most accessible career opportunities available in India right now. And this guide from Code Of School is going to walk you through exactly how to step into it.

What Social Media Marketing Actually Means Professionally

Let us clear something up before we go further. Social media marketing as a career is not about posting pretty pictures or writing clever captions, although those are part of it. It is a strategic, data driven discipline that sits at the intersection of creativity, psychology, brand strategy, and digital analytics.

A professional social media marketer is responsible for building and managing a brand's presence across platforms, creating content that drives real engagement, running paid social campaigns that generate leads and sales, monitoring audience behaviour and platform algorithm changes, and reporting on performance metrics that connect to actual business goals.

It requires creative thinking on one hand and analytical rigour on the other. The professionals who are exceptional at this combination are among the most sought after people in India's digital marketing industry.

The Career Roles Available in Social Media Marketing

The field is broader than most people assume when they first explore it. Here are the distinct roles that exist under the social media marketing umbrella:

Social Media Executive

The entry point for most professionals. Responsibilities include content creation, scheduling, community management, and basic performance reporting. This is where you build your foundational skills and learn how different platforms behave.

Social Media Manager

A mid level role where you own the strategy for one or multiple brands. You are not just executing, you are planning, leading content calendars, briefing designers and writers, and owning the performance metrics.

Paid Social Specialist

A specialisation focused entirely on paid advertising on platforms like Meta Ads Manager and LinkedIn Campaign Manager. This is one of the higher paying niches within social media marketing because it is directly tied to revenue generation.

Content Strategist

A role that sits across social media and broader content marketing. You plan what content to create, for which audience, on which platform, and at which stage of the customer journey.

Social Media Analyst

A data focused role where you track performance across platforms, identify trends, build reports, and translate numbers into strategic recommendations.

Brand Community Manager

A growing role focused on building and nurturing online communities around a brand, most commonly seen in e-commerce, gaming, and lifestyle sectors.

Influencer Marketing Manager

Responsible for identifying, contacting, briefing, and managing relationships with influencers and content creators across niches and platform sizes.

The Skills That Actually Get You Hired

Understanding what the job involves is one thing. Knowing what skills you need to build is another. Here is what social media marketing employers are genuinely evaluating when they hire:

  • Platform fluency: You need to understand how Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube, and X actually work, not just as a user but as a marketer. Algorithm behaviour, content formats, audience targeting, and platform specific best practices all matter.
  • Content creation: Strong written communication, basic graphic design skills using tools like Canva, script writing for short form video, and an eye for visual storytelling are all expectations at even junior levels.
  • Paid advertising: Meta Ads Manager and LinkedIn Campaign Manager are the two platforms you must be comfortable with. Understanding campaign objectives, audience targeting, budget allocation, ad creative testing, and ROAS optimisation separates candidates immediately.
  • Analytics and reporting: Google Analytics, Meta Insights, LinkedIn Analytics, and platform native reporting tools. The ability to look at data, identify what it means, and articulate it clearly to a client or manager is one of the most valuable skills a social media marketer can have.
  • Strategy and planning: Content calendars, campaign planning, audience research, and competitive analysis. Employers want people who can think ahead, not just react.
  • Community management: Responding to comments, managing DMs, handling negative feedback professionally, and nurturing an engaged following all require people skills that algorithms cannot replace.

The One Thing That Separates Professionals From Hobbyists

Anyone can post content. Not everyone can build an audience, run a profitable ad campaign, analyse what is working, and connect their work to measurable business outcomes. The professionals who earn well in social media marketing are the ones who can show results.

A portfolio that demonstrates follower growth, engagement rate improvements, successful paid campaigns, and clear reporting is worth more than any certificate. Which is why the quality of your training matters so much. Watching tutorials and reading articles will give you information. Structured, hands on training under experienced mentors will give you skills that employers can see and verify.
